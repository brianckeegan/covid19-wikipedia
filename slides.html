A Quantitative Portrait of Wikipedia's Collaborations during the COVID-19 Pandemic

Brian Keegan

28 April 2021

Summary

Longitudinal analysis of Wikipedia's coverage of COVID-19 through 2020

Revision histories and activities reveal information production dynamics

Pageview data reveals information seeking and consumption dynamics

Content of articles can be used to reconstruct hyperlink networks and measure content similarity

(Old) pre-print on arXiv

Background

Wikipedia's information production and consumption about current events is a rich empirical setting

The disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic are encoded in Wikipedia article behavior

Methods

Wikipedia's APIs expose historical data about article revision activity, content, and pageviews

Custom Python scripts to retrieve and analyze data

Data

Start with high-relevance "seed articles" about pandemic event, disease, and virus

Category-related articles: Members of Category:SARS-CoV-2 including COVID-19

Neighbor-related articles: Articles ever hyperlinked from the seed articles (e.g., Donald Trump)
